Practice Manager and Connect Interfaces

A CONTRELATION record will be created in any of the following circumstances:

  • indirectly, whenever a new private client is created
  • indirectly, whenever an individual contact is added as a party to a private Client
  • whenever a relationship is created either side of which is an individual contact

Before creating a new client the client must already be an individual contact. It follows that the creation of a private client produces a CONTRELATIONS record for the original contact record pointing to its own new CLIDB record. Similarly a CLIERELATIONS record is created at the same time for the new client record, pointing to its own original CONTACTS record.

Field Notes

Maintenance of ROL-CODE field

ROL-CODE has a user maintainable value only when you create relationships manually from the relationship tab of a Client or Contact record. You will already have a table of ROLES created in the ROLES maintenance program and this will be shown as a drop down list from which you must make a selection.

Special values for ROL-CODE of "C*"

If the related party is a client then ROL-CODE does not have a normal user-assigned or maintainable value in a CONTRELATIONS record. In that case ROL-CODE will have special value of "C*" inaccessible to the user. This is the common situation wherever a solitary client is created from an existing contact record or where a contact is added as a joint party to a client record. In the case of multiple parties each and every party will have a CONTRELATIONS record with a ROL-CODE of "C*".

Special reciprocal values for ROL-CODE of "M*"

The reciprocal record in this situation will be a minimum of one CLIERELATIONS record where the CL-CODE field is the client’s code and the ROL-CODE has another special value of "M*" inaccessible to the user. There will be a further CLIERELATIONS record with a ROL-CODE of "M*" pointing to each and every contact added as a party to the Client.
