Interface Notes

This table is updated as a by-product of transactions paying off a supplier invoice, and is not directly editable. It is very similar in design to the PAYMENTS table but data values are much simpler.


Field Notes

SUPP-PAYMEMNTS.TRANS-NO and ENTRY-NO together identify a unique preceding entry in NOMTRANS for a purchase ledger transaction whether a CI on a nominal ledger account or an AD/ADV/ADVU on a client matter.

SUPP-PAYMENTS.PAYMENT-TYPE can only have the value NP.

SUPP-PAYMENTS.PAYMENT-TXNO will invariably be larger than TRANS-NO and identifies another and later entry in NOMTRANS which is paying off the TRANS-NO transaction. It is invariably a NP posting.


Linking And Application

Bill Payments

SUPP-PAYMENTS is used to identify purchase ledger invoices and payments thereof and for this purpose is usually linked to NOMTRANS to identify both the bills and the payments. The method for this is to open NOMTRANS twice, and give each instance of the table an appropriate alias name e.g. invoiceNOMTRANS and paymentNOMTRANS.

The linkage would then be like this:

invoiceNOMTRANS . TRANS-NO -> SUPP-PAYMENTS.TRANS-NO
invoiceNOMTRANS.ENTRY-NO -> SUPP-PAYMENTS.ENTRY-NO
SUPP-PAYMENTS.PAYMENT-TXNO -> paymentNOMTRANS.TRANS-NO

Your report's Record Selection Formula might include a condition such as:

invoiceNOMTRANS.POSTING-TYPE[1 to 2]="AD"

to confine the report to entries on client matters, or:

invoiceNOMTRANS.POSTING-TYPE = "CI"

(for entries on nominal accounts only)
