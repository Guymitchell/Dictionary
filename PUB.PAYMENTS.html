User Interface Notes

This table is updated as a by-product of a wide variety of transactions that credit the office side of a client matter ledger, and is not directly editable by you. As you will see below it is not confined to actual payments but embraces credit notes, write-offs and allocations of existing office credits.

It also includes the JTU posting type: these are entries generated automatically by the software when you post the Transfer Journal and represent the aggregate value of the individual postings that you set as Journal entries (rather than Execute) at original posting time.


Field Notes

TRANS-NO and ENTRY-NO together identify a unique preceding entry in NOMTRANS such as part of a BC posting e.g. one particular Fee Earner's share of profit costs.

PAYMENTS-TXNO will invariably be larger than TRANS-NO and identifies another and later entry in NOMTRANS which is paying off the TRANS-NO transaction. However, it does not pinpoint exactly which entry constitutes the payment.

Linking and Application

Bill Payments

PAYMENTS is generally used to identify bills to clients and payments thereof and for this purpose is usually linked to NOMTRANS to identify both the bills and the payments. The method for this is to open NOMTRANS twice, and give each instance of the table an appropriate alias name e.g. billingNOMTRANS and paymentNOMTRANS.

The linkage would then be like this:

billingNOMTRANS.TRANS-NO -> PAYMENTS.TRANS-NO
billingNOMTRANS.ENTRY-NO -> PAYMENTS.ENTRY-NO
PAYMENTS.PAYMENT-TXNO -> paymentNOMTRANS.TRANS-NO

Your report's Record Selection Formula might include a condition such as: BillingNOMTRANS.POSTING-TYPE = "BC"

Since PAYMENTS has indexes for both originating and paying transaction numbers you can effectively make either billingNOMTRANS or paymentNOMTRANS your "primary" table.


Client to Office Transfers

Another use might be to list your Transfer Journal updates showing all the individual inter-ledger transfers comprised in each. This could be useful to verify your office to client transfers, for example. In this example giving your NOMTRANS the alias names transferNOMTRANS and jtuNOMTRANS:

transferNOMTRANS.TRANS-NO -> PAYMENTS.TRANS-NO
transferNOMTRANS.ENTRY-NO -> PAYMENTS.ENTRY-NO
PAYMENTS.PAYMENT-TXNO -> jtuNOMTRANS.TRANS-NO

Your report's Record Selection Formula might include a condition such as:

(transferNOMTRANS.POSTING-TYPE="CO" or transferNOMTRANS.POSTING-TYPE = "OC") and
PAYMENTS.PAYMENT-TYPE = "JTU"
